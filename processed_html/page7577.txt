What do you mean by multi-core processors? Multi-core processors are an integral part of how fast devices work and how efficient they are. For instance, a dual-core processor is a CPU with two separate cores, whereas a quad-core CPU has four separate CPUs with their own resources. If quad-core CPUs are just now beginning to become the standard - let's now welcome hexa or a CPU equivalent of six single core processors.
