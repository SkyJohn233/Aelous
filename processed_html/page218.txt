(Photo: Stan Carpenter / The Times) Buy Photo Even though their respective schools finished behind Captain Shreve in the District 1-5A race, Parkway and Haughton athletes swept top honors in the recently released All-District 1-5A team. Parkway senior Eric Williams, a Southern-Baton Rouge signee, captured MVP honors after hitting .385 with 7 home runs and 36 RBIs for the league runners-up. Senior Nick Heckman, a Nicholls State signee, was dominant on the mound for the Bucs with an 0.48 ERA and 34 strikeouts in 29 innings. He defeated both Shreve and Parkway during the 2018 season. Haughton ' s Nick Heckman prepares to throw a pitch. (Photo: Andrea Heckman/Contributed photo) Captain Shreve coach Todd Sharp landed Coach of the Year honors after taking his team to the LHSAA Class 5A state quarterfinals. The Gators were 34-6 overall and 10-2 in the district. Additional pitchers named to the first team were Airline senior Jerry Couch (1.85 ERA), and Shreve seniors Reid DuBois (2.30 ERA) and Stephen Smith (1.54 ERA). >Infielders recognized for their play were juniors Caleb Reese (.359), Eric Brown (.310) and Amani Larry (.378) of Parkway, DuBois (.361) and senior Noah Gondran (.463) of Evangel. Outfielders on the first team are Byrd junior Charlie Barham (.367), Shreve senior Anthony Stallcup (.370), Haughton senior Lake Lowery (.381) and Williams.<br >Byrd s Julien Deleon (.343) is the all-district designated hitter. Utility players named to the first team were sophomore Blake Shapen of Evangel (.343), senior Christopher Noble of Shreve (.310), freshman Peyton Stovall of Haughton (.394) and senior Derious Bradford of Parkway (.324).
